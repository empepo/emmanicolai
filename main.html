About Me

I'm an aerospace engineer, working on my Master's in Aeronautics and Astronautics at Stanford after my Bachelor's in Aerospace Engineering at Queen Mary University of London. Over the course of my studies, I've focused on orbital dynamics, drawn to the precision and logic behind how spacecraft move and behave in orbit. Alongside the technical side, I've taken on leadership roles in multiple teams, where I've learned that strong engineering is as much about people and direction as it is about equations.

My work sits at the intersection of technical rigour and long-term thinking. I'm interested not only in how we put spacecraft into orbit, but whether we should — and how we do it responsibly. Space sustainability and policy matter just as much to me as control laws and propagation models. If we're going to build in orbit, we need to clean up after ourselves too.

Right now, I'm focused on orbital dynamics, debris mitigation, and in-space manufacturing concepts that aim to close the loop on materials in orbit. Ultimately, I want to contribute to a future where space exploration is ambitious, technically excellent, and sustainable by design.
